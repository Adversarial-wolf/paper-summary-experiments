# 💡 Simple Explanation: C-Level GenAI Readiness

## 🏗️ The Analogy: The Fancy House with No Alarm System
Imagine a company that has spent millions building a stunning, futuristic glass mansion. They have the best architects, the finest furniture, and a great set of house rules (the "Upstream Readiness"). 

**The Problem:**
They forgot to install a single security camera or alarm system. They have no idea who is entering the house, what they are doing, or if someone is stealing the silver in real-time. They only find out something is wrong *after* the house has been robbed (the "Runtime Gap").

## 🗣️ In Plain English
Many companies are rushing to use AI. They are good at the "planning" phase (making rules, classifying data), but they are completely blind once the AI is actually running. They don't have the tools to see a prompt injection happening in real-time or to track if an AI agent is going rogue.

## 🌍 Why It Matters
Having a "policy" on AI is not the same as having "security" for AI. This paper warns executives that they are currently "not ready" because they are focusing on the paperwork rather than the actual monitoring tools (AI-EDR) needed to stop attacks.
